Problem Statement

Customer churn is a major challenge for telecom companies because losing existing customers directly impacts revenue and business growth. The goal of this project is to build a machine learning classification model that predicts whether a customer is likely to leave the telecom service based on factors such as tenure, monthly charges, contract type, internet services, payment method, and customer support features.

The system analyzes historical customer data and classifies customers into:

likely to churn
likely to stay

This prediction can help telecom companies:

identify at-risk customers
improve retention strategies
offer targeted promotions
reduce customer loss

The project uses supervised machine learning techniques including:

data preprocessing
categorical encoding
train-test splitting
Logistic Regression
classification evaluation metrics such as accuracy, precision, recall, and confusion matrix.
